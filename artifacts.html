Historical Artifacts

Objects and documents that tell the story of independence

Preserving History Through Objects

The story of Israel's independence lives not only in books and memories, but also in the physical objects, documents, and artifacts that have survived from that extraordinary period. These tangible pieces of history connect us directly to the events and people who shaped the nation's birth.

From the original Declaration of Independence scroll to personal items belonging to the founding fathers, from military equipment used in the 1948 war to everyday objects from the early days of statehood, these artifacts provide an intimate window into Israel's founding era.

This collection showcases some of the most significant artifacts from Israel's independence period, housed in museums and private collections around the world. Each item tells a unique story and helps us understand the human experience behind the historical events.

Key Documents

The Declaration of Independence Scroll

The most important artifact of Israel's independence is the original scroll of the Declaration of Independence, handwritten in Hebrew and signed by 37 leaders on May 14, 1948. This parchment document, carefully preserved in the Israel State Archives, represents the formal birth certificate of the modern State of Israel.

Learn more about the scroll's creation

The scroll was handwritten by Moshe Kol using a special calligraphy pen. The parchment was imported from Europe specifically for this purpose. Due to time constraints, only 37 of the intended 51 members of the Provisional Council were able to sign on that historic day.

The scroll measures 52 cm wide and 38 cm high, and is written entirely in Hebrew using traditional Jewish scribal techniques.

The First Israeli Flag

The flag that flew over the Tel Aviv Museum during the independence ceremony was one of the first official Israeli flags. Based on the design of the Zionist flag created in 1891, it features the blue Star of David between two blue stripes on a white background.

Personal Artifacts

Ben-Gurion's Personal Items

David Ben-Gurion's personal artifacts from the independence period provide intimate glimpses into the life of Israel's founding father. These items, housed in the Ben-Gurion House in Tel Aviv, include his desk, books, and personal correspondence from the crucial years of 1947-1948.
