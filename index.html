Echoes of Time: Israel's Independence 1948

A digital journey through the birth of a nation

Welcome to Our Digital Museum

On May 14, 1948, David Ben-Gurion proclaimed the establishment of the State of Israel, marking the end of nearly 2,000 years of Jewish diaspora and the beginning of a new chapter in Middle Eastern history. This moment came after decades of struggle, hope, and determination.

This digital exhibition takes you through four crucial aspects of Israel's independence story. You'll discover the historical events that led to statehood, meet the key figures who shaped this momentous period, explore the artifacts that tell this story, and understand its lasting impact on the world.

The declaration may have taken just a few minutes to read, but its echoes continue to resonate through time, representing the fulfillment of an ancient dream and the beginning of modern Israel.

Quick Overview

Independence in Numbers

  • Date: May 14, 1948 (5th of Iyar, 5708)
  • Population: Approximately 650,000 Jews
  • Area: 20,770 square kilometers
  • Years of Mandate: 28 years (1920-1948)
  • UN Resolution 181: November 29, 1947

"The State of Israel will be open for Jewish immigration and for the Ingathering of the Exiles."

- Declaration of Independence, May 14, 1948
